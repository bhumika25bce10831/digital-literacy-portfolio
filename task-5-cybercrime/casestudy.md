# Phishing Case Study

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. It usually occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to be from trusted organizations.

For example, a college student receives an email that looks like it is from their bank. The email asks them to update their account details urgently and provides a link. The link leads to a fake website that looks exactly like the real bank website. When the student enters their login credentials, the attacker captures the information.

Phishing attacks often target students, working professionals, and elderly people who may not be aware of such scams. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.

This cybercrime is dangerous because it exploits human trust rather than technical weaknesses. Therefore, awareness and caution are essential to prevent such attacks.
